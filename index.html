🟡 New team target: $3,000–$8,000 IP/mo
✅ Healthy IP rate: 40–60% of AP written

Proof That It's Possible
🧱
David Goggins
Navy SEAL · Ultra-Athlete
Failed the SEAL entrance test twice. Went from 297 lbs and working as an exterminator to becoming one of the most decorated special operations soldiers in American history. At age 24, he started with nothing.
"You are in danger of living a life so comfortable and soft that you will die without ever realizing your true potential."
🏊
Diana Nyad
Open Water Swimmer
At 64 years old — after four failed attempts over 35 years — she swam 110 miles from Cuba to Florida with no shark cage in 53 hours. She was stung by jellyfish the entire way and kept swimming.
"Find a way. Never, ever give up."
🚀
Elon Musk
First-Generation Builder
SpaceX's first three rockets exploded. He had $30M left — the cost of exactly one more launch. He launched it. It worked. Tesla was weeks from bankruptcy. He put in his last dollar. Both companies survived.
"When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor."
🥊
Muhammad Ali
The Greatest
Stripped of his title, banned from boxing for 3.5 years during the prime of his career for refusing military draft. Came back to reclaim the heavyweight championship twice. Refused to let the system define his ceiling.
"I am the greatest. I said that even before I knew I was."
📚
J.K. Rowling
Author · Builder from Zero
A single mother on welfare, clinically depressed, living in a café in Edinburgh writing on napkins. Harry Potter was rejected by 12 publishers. She kept submitting. That manuscript became a $25B franchise.
"Rock bottom became the solid foundation on which I rebuilt my life."
🧗
Alex Honnold
Free Solo Climber
In 2017, he climbed 3,000 feet of sheer vertical granite — El Capitan — with no rope, no safety gear, and no second chances. Every move had to be perfect. He trained for years and executed flawlessly.
"The point isn't to conquer the wall. It's to become the person who can."
🏃
Roger Bannister
The 4-Minute Mile
Scientists said a human could not run a mile in under 4 minutes — physiologically impossible. On May 6, 1954, Bannister did it in 3:59.4. Within one year, 37 others broke the barrier. The limit was never physical.
"The man who can drive himself further once the effort gets painful is the man who will win."
💼
Les Brown
From Homeless to $10M Speaker
Labeled "educably mentally retarded" as a child. Slept on the floor of his office when he started his business. Called radio stations every single day until they gave him a time slot. Built a speaking empire from nothing.
"Someone's opinion of you does not have to become your reality."
🌍
Nelson Mandela
27 Years. Unbroken.
Spent 27 years in prison on Robben Island — 18 years in a 7-foot cell, breaking rocks daily. Walked out in 1990 and led South Africa to democracy. He emerged with more dignity than when he entered.
"It always seems impossible until it's done."

Teams Win Championships

The Work Is the Meaning

Momentum Is Contagious
