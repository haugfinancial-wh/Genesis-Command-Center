SCRIPTS & REBUTTALS

Tap any objection to reveal the response
"Hi, is this [Name]? Hey [Name] — this is [Your Name] calling. I work with farmers and agricultural families in [area] helping them make sure their family is protected if something unexpected happens. Quick question — do you currently have any life insurance coverage in place, or is that something that's kind of been on the back burner?"
Key: Lead with their identity. Keep it under 20 seconds. Ask ONE question and stop talking. Silence is your friend.
"Hi [Name] — this is [Your Name]. You recently purchased a home in [area] — congratulations on that. I specialize in mortgage protection insurance, which ensures your family keeps the home if something unexpected happens to you. Do you have a few minutes to see if you'd even qualify?"
Key: They're emotionally invested in the home. "Qualify" language lowers resistance.
"Hi [Name] — this is [Your Name]. You had previously looked into a life insurance plan through Ethos. I'm following up to see if you ever got that squared away or if life just got in the way. Do you have 5 minutes?"
Key: They already showed intent. Remove the friction — make it feel easy.
"Hi [Name] — this is [Your Name]. At some point you expressed interest in life insurance for your family. I know that was a while back — things come up. I just want to make sure you're not still going without protection. Is now a decent time to chat?"
Key: Be warm and human. They wanted coverage — life got in the way. Make it easy to say yes now.
"Hey [Name] — it's [Your Name] again. We spoke [X days ago] about protecting your family. I didn't want to leave you without getting this in front of you properly. Do you have 5 minutes today or is later this week better?"
Key: Most income lives in follow-ups #3–5. Always give two time options, never a yes/no.
Objections — Tap to Reveal
"I'm not interested." ▼
"Totally fair — most people aren't until they see the numbers. I'm not here to sell you anything today. I just want to see if you'd even qualify. Takes 3 minutes. Would that be alright?"
"I already have insurance." ▼
"That's great — most people I talk to do. I'm not here to replace what you have. I just want to make sure you're not overpaying and there are no gaps. Would you be open to a free second opinion?"
"I need to talk to my spouse." ▼
"Absolutely — this affects both of you. Can we schedule a time to go over it together? I can do a quick 20-minute call with both of you. What day works — Tuesday or Thursday?"
"I can't afford it." ▼
"I understand — that's exactly why I want to show you the options before assuming. Some plans start under $30 a month. Can I at least show you what's available so you can make an informed decision?"
"Just send me something." ▼
"I can do that — but the mailers never do it justice. What I'd rather do is take 5 minutes right now to walk you through the key points so you actually understand what you're looking at. Does that work?"
"I had a bad experience before." ▼
"I'm sorry to hear that — that's more common than it should be. I only work with carriers I'd put my own family on. Can I show you the difference? 10 minutes."
"Call me back next month." ▼
"Of course — I'll do that. Can I ask though, is there a specific reason you'd rather wait? Health rating factors can affect your options, and locking something in now can actually save you money long term."

Proof That It's Possible
🧱
David Goggins
Navy SEAL · Ultra-Athlete
Failed the SEAL entrance test twice. Went from 297 lbs and working as an exterminator to becoming one of the most decorated special operations soldiers in American history. At age 24, he started with nothing.
"You are in danger of living a life so comfortable and soft that you will die without ever realizing your true potential."
🏊
Diana Nyad
Open Water Swimmer
At 64 years old — after four failed attempts over 35 years — she swam 110 miles from Cuba to Florida with no shark cage in 53 hours. She was stung by jellyfish the entire way and kept swimming.
"Find a way. Never, ever give up."
🚀
Elon Musk
First-Generation Builder
SpaceX's first three rockets exploded. He had $30M left — the cost of exactly one more launch. He launched it. It worked. Tesla was weeks from bankruptcy. He put in his last dollar. Both companies survived.
"When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor."
🥊
Muhammad Ali
The Greatest
Stripped of his title, banned from boxing for 3.5 years during the prime of his career for refusing military draft. Came back to reclaim the heavyweight championship twice. Refused to let the system define his ceiling.
"I am the greatest. I said that even before I knew I was."
📚
J.K. Rowling
Author · Builder from Zero
A single mother on welfare, clinically depressed, living in a café in Edinburgh writing on napkins. Harry Potter was rejected by 12 publishers. She kept submitting. That manuscript became a $25B franchise.
"Rock bottom became the solid foundation on which I rebuilt my life."
🧗
Alex Honnold
Free Solo Climber
In 2017, he climbed 3,000 feet of sheer vertical granite — El Capitan — with no rope, no safety gear, and no second chances. Every move had to be perfect. He trained for years and executed flawlessly.
"The point isn't to conquer the wall. It's to become the person who can."
🏃
Roger Bannister
The 4-Minute Mile
Scientists said a human could not run a mile in under 4 minutes — physiologically impossible. On May 6, 1954, Bannister did it in 3:59.4. Within one year, 37 others broke the barrier. The limit was never physical.
"The man who can drive himself further once the effort gets painful is the man who will win."
💼
Les Brown
From Homeless to $10M Speaker
Labeled "educably mentally retarded" as a child. Slept on the floor of his office when he started his business. Called radio stations every single day until they gave him a time slot. Built a speaking empire from nothing.
"Someone's opinion of you does not have to become your reality."
🌍
Nelson Mandela
27 Years. Unbroken.
Spent 27 years in prison on Robben Island — 18 years in a 7-foot cell, breaking rocks daily. Walked out in 1990 and led South Africa to democracy. He emerged with more dignity than when he entered.
"It always seems impossible until it's done."
